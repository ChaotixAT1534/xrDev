In "Hamlet on the Holodeck" by Janet H. Murray, the author talks about his experience with coding. Janet teaches in MIT since 1971 and did not go back to coding until early 1980s. Janet loves literature and she finds coding interesting because it is like learning a new language. This is true because from my experience on coding HTML, I would have to find the base structure of HTML before you can include any text. Janet says that we are living in a historic transition where cyberliterature is applied to many websites, video games, and more. I find it interesting that literature is everywhere because I play a lot of video games and there is always a story when it comes to characters or world building. There can be a story behind the towns you go in games or a story on the weapon you recieve in a game. It is really amazing how literature impacts world building.
